City of Toronto Open Data
Green Streets

Depicts public roads incorporating green infrastructure. These projects utilize solar reflectivity, photocatalytic paving, and soil cells to improve air quality and reduce extreme rainfall impacts.

Green Roofs

Includes structures with vegetated surfaces or cool roof materials. These installations reflect sunlight and manage stormwater, significantly reducing building energy costs.

Green Spaces

Locations of parks, ravines, and cemeteries. Beyond recreation, these areas provide critical ecosystem benefits and natural cooling relief.

Academic Research
Heat Vulnerability Index

A comprehensive PCA-based index combining heat exposure (2010-2019), population sensitivity (age, canopy cover), and adaptive capacity. This dataset identifies neighborhoods disproportionately affected by rising temperatures.

Data Usage

These datasets are integrated using Mapbox GL JS and Turf.js to provide real-time spatial analysis and visualization of Toronto's environmental health.

Methodology

The Heat Vulnerability index uses standard deviation (z-scores) to rank regions from Low to High intensity based on decade-long thermal patterns.
